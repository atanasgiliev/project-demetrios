Hiatus Detector

Χασμῳδία

Identifies and categorizes every instance of three types of vowel hiatus across an uploaded Ancient Greek text file.

What it does

Hiatus is one of the most studied phenomena in Greek verse euphony. Ancient metricians distinguished between permissible hiatus and infelicitous sequences that poets typically avoided through elision, crasis, or rearrangement. Detecting and tabulating hiatus across a long poem or corpus is a laborious task by hand; this tool automates it.

The detector handles Unicode Greek text with full polytonic diacritics, correctly distinguishing true diphthongs from vowel sequences resolved into two syllables by diaeresis (ἀΐδηλος), and a variety of other edge cases and scenarios.

How to use it

  1. Upload a UTF-8 encoded .txt file of Greek text.
  2. Select the hiatus types to detect (standard, at verse-end, etc.).
  3. Click Run Detector.
  4. Download the annotated HTML output and/or the CSV occurrence table.

Anaphora & Epiphora Detector

Ἀναφορά

Detects and highlights rhetorical repetition (anaphora, epiphora, and word repetition across clause boundaries) in Ancient Greek verse, with fully configurable phrase length, distance windows, and normalisation options.

What it does

Anaphora, the repetition of the same word or phrase at the beginning of successive verse lines, and epiphora (antistrophe), repetition at line-ends, are among the most powerful structural devices in Greek poetry, extensively discussed by ancient rhetorical theorists from Gorgias onward. This tool makes their systematic identification possible across extended texts.

A third mode, word repetition, detects phrase-initial repetition across clause boundaries delimited by punctuation (. · ; ,), capturing figures that recur across periods within and between lines.

Stop-words (particles, articles, prepositions) are handled with philological care: they are "transparent" — skipped when determining the comparison phrase, but noted in the output table, so the scholar can see exactly what was elided from the matching logic. All normalisation steps (accent stripping, breathing removal, iota subscript, elision, nu-movable) are individually toggleable.

Detection modes

Anaphora — Gold highlight
Repetition at the start of consecutive verse lines
Epiphora — Teal highlight
Repetition at the end of consecutive verse lines
Word Repetition — Violet highlight
Clause-initial repetition across punctuation boundaries

Technical Architecture

Scholarship without
infrastructure

Every Project Demetrios tool runs entirely in the browser using Pyodide, a WebAssembly port of CPython. This means the full power of Python's unicodedata module is available for precise polytonic Greek handling, with no server, no API key, and no data ever leaving your machine.

Outputs are always download-ready: annotated HTML for publication or presentation, and CSV for statistical analysis in R, Python, or a spreadsheet.

1

Upload or Paste

Load any UTF-8 encoded Greek text — a single ode, a full epic, a papyrus fragment.

2

Configure

Set detection options: phrase length, distance window, normalisation, stop-word handling.

3

Analyse

Python runs locally in your browser via Pyodide. No data leaves your device.

4

Export

Download an annotated HTML file or a CSV occurrence table for further analysis.
