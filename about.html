Project Description

Why Demetrios?

The project takes its name from Demetrios of Phaleron (c. 350–280 BCE), the Athenian orator, statesman, and philosopher who may have played a key role in the founding of the Library of Alexandria. A student of Theophrastus and associate of the Peripatetic school, Demetrios, was among the most important literary critics and rhetorical theorists of antiquity. He is the probable author of Περὶ Ἑρμηνείας (On Style), the systematic treatise on Greek prose style and rhetorical figures.

We chose his name as our patron because his intellectual project, to collect, preserve, and systematically analyse the texts of Greek literature, mirrors our own in a digital register. Where he assembled papyrus scrolls, we assemble algorithms; where he catalogued authorship, we map phonological and rhetorical patterns.

Our tools are designed to assist scholars in the close reading of Greek poetry. We do not replace the philologist's judgement but rather attempt to augment it, making visible patterns that lie beneath the threshold of unaided attention over long stretches of text.
