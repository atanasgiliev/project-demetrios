Project Demetrios is a collaborative research initiative at the intersection of classical philology and digital humanities, committed to open-access scholarship in the analysis of Ancient Greek verse.

Project Description

Why Demetrios?

The project takes its name from Demetrius of Phaleron (c. 350–280 BCE), the Athenian orator, statesman, and philosopher who became the first director of the Library of Alexandria. A student of Theophrastus and associate of the Peripatetic school, Demetrius was among the most important literary critics and rhetorical theorists of antiquity — the probable author of Περὶ Ἑρμηνείας (On Style), the earliest surviving systematic treatise on Greek prose style and rhetorical figures.

We choose his name as our patron because his intellectual project — to collect, preserve, and systematically analyse the texts of Greek literature — mirrors our own in a digital register. Where he assembled papyrus scrolls, we assemble algorithms; where he catalogued authorship, we map phonological and rhetorical patterns.

Our tools are designed to assist scholars in the close reading of Greek poetry. We do not replace the philologist's judgement — we attempt to augment it, making visible patterns that lie beneath the threshold of unaided attention over long stretches of text.

Principal Investigator

Atanas G. Iliev

Dartmouth College · University of Oxford (Univ)

Atanas is a Classicist completing his undergraduate degree at Dartmouth College and proceeding to read Classics at University College, Oxford. His research centres on Greek phonology, verse euphony, and the quantitative analysis of sound patterns in Archaic and Classical poetry. He is the principal developer of the Project Demetrios toolkit.
